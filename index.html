Rules

In the Game of Life, the population of cubes evolves over time. In each generation, whether a cube lives or dies depends on its neighbors.

More specifically, choose four numbers a, b, c, and d. Here's the breakdown:

  1. If a cube has between a and b neighbors, it stays alive to the next generation. Otherwise it dies (by over- or underpopulation.)
  2. If an empty cell has between c and d neighbors, it becomes alive in the next generation (by reproduction).

Because these four parameters determine the rules of the game, for fixed a, b, c, and d, we say that you are playing Life version a,b,c,d. Note that each cube can have at least 0 neighbors, and at most 26.

Modes
